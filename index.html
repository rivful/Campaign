    The origin of the domestic dog includes the dog's evolutionary divergence from the wolf, its domestication, and its development into dog types and dog breeds

    The dog is a member of the genus Canis, which forms part of the wolf-like canids, and was the first species and the only large carnivore to have been domesticated

    The dog and the extant gray wolf are sister taxa, as modern wolves are not closely related to the population of wolves that was first domesticated

    Step 2

    Domestic dogs have been selectively bred for millennia for various behaviors, sensory capabilities, and physical attributes

    Modern dog breeds show more variation in size, appearance, and behavior than any other domestic animal
